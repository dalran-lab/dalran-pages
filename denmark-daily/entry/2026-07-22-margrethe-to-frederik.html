A Queen Steps Down — From Margrethe II to Frederik X

Denmark's monarchy is among the oldest continuous lines in the world, traceable back to Gorm the Old in the 10th century — the same king remembered on the Jelling stones. For most living Danes, though, the crown had a single face: Queen Margrethe II, who acceded in 1972 on the death of her father, Frederik IX, and reigned for 52 years.

Then, in her televised New Year's Eve address on 31 December 2023, she did something no Danish monarch had done voluntarily in nearly 900 years — since Eric III in 1146. She announced she would abdicate. On 14 January 2024, the anniversary of her accession, she signed the declaration, and her elder son became King Frederik X.

Margrethe was never a remote figurehead. A trained archaeologist who studied at Cambridge and the Sorbonne, she illustrated an edition of Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings under a pseudonym, designed stage sets and church vestments, and was for decades a famously heavy smoker before quitting in public view. Her wit and cultural seriousness made her one of Europe's most respected sovereigns.

Her successor arrives with a modern story. Frederik met Mary Donaldson, an Australian marketing executive, at a Sydney pub during the 2000 Olympics; she is now Queen Mary, Denmark's first Australian-born queen. The King's chosen motto — "United, committed, for the Kingdom of Denmark" — signals continuity over reinvention.

The role itself is strictly ceremonial. Under the Constitution of 1849 and its revisions, the monarch reigns but does not rule; real power rests with the elected Folketing and the government. What the crown supplies is symbolism and continuity — and, in Denmark's case, remarkable popularity.

You can watch it up close in Copenhagen. The royal family's winter residence is Amalienborg, four rococo palaces around an octagonal square, where the Royal Life Guards (Den Kongelige Livgarde) perform the changing of the guard daily, marching from Rosenborg Castle. When the monarch is in residence, the royal standard flies above the roof.
